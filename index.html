Who I Am

Academic profile and research identity.

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Radio Electronics, Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Communication, Brno University of Technology, focused on applied AI for wireless systems and data-driven engineering problems.

What I Do

Core technical themes and methods.

I design and evaluate machine learning and neural network models for wireless communications, signal analysis, and practical AI deployment. Recent topics include occupancy detection, multipath component detection in power delay profiles, and predictive maintenance for 5G and rail systems.

What I Work On

Selected projects and direct responsibilities.

TACR TREND - Thermal Dilatation AI (2022-2023)

AI for compensation of machining thermal dilatations (TA CR, TREND). Role: data analysis, visualization, and neural network modeling for prediction from machine sensor streams (temperature and rotation data).

Applied AI Predictive Modeling Industrial Data

5GRailCare (2025-2027)

Innovative remote 5G management of railway fleets with predictive maintenance estimation. Role: data analysis, visualization, and model development for locomotive maintenance prediction using neural networks.

5G Predictive Maintenance Neural Networks

Open RAN Cybersecurity (2024-2025)

Open RAN cybersecurity project focused on identity theft of base stations and 5G physical layer verification. Role: framework development for optimization and testing of fake base station presence classifiers.

Open RAN 5G Security ML Classification

GACR - mmWave Propagation AI (2023-2025)

Multi-band mmWave propagation prediction for dynamic and static scenarios. Role: design of neural network and machine learning models for multipath component identification in Power Delay Profiles (PDP).

mmWave PDP Analysis Deep Learning
